01

Motivation

The Problem — Robots Are Blind to Humans

Traditional robots have powerful sensing — cameras, LIDAR, force sensors, proximity sensors. They understand the physical environment very well. But they are completely blind to the humans around them.

A robot has no idea if the person nearby is stressed or calm, focused or distracted, comfortable or uncomfortable. This project adds a social sensing layer — the robot gains emotional context awareness about the humans around it.

Goal: Move from task-aware robots to context-aware robots.

Why Emotion-Aware Manipulators?

Manipulators already communicate through motion — humans naturally read intent from how an arm moves. Fast feels aggressive. Slow feels safe. Jerky feels broken. But this communication is accidental. The arm has no idea what it's expressing.

When a manipulator gains emotional awareness, three things happen:

01

It Becomes Safer

It can sense human discomfort or stress and slow down or stop — no buttons needed. The robot adapts its speed and behavior based on the person's emotional state, creating an intrinsic safety layer beyond physical sensors.

02

It Becomes Context-Aware

It adapts its behavior based on the situation — gentle in calm moments, cautious in tense ones. The same task can be performed differently depending on the human's emotional state, making the robot truly situation-adaptive.

03

It Becomes a Companion

It responds to people, shows personality, and feels alive — not just a machine executing commands. Emotion turns a tool into a teammate.

02

Use Cases

Emotion-aware manipulation opens up applications across industries where robots interact with humans:

HEALTHCARE
Medical / Surgical Assist
Detects surgeon's focus and stress level. Adjusts responsiveness to situation urgency. A stressed surgeon gets slower, more predictable robot motion.
MANUFACTURING
Collaborative Robotics
Robot adapts speed when the worker is stressed. Expressive motion signals intent, making human-robot interaction safer in shared workspaces.
ENTERTAINMENT
Chess Playing Robot
Reads opponent's emotions to gain situational awareness like a real player. Adapts playing style and body language based on confidence or frustration.
CONSUMER
Companion / Desk Pet
A desk companion with personality (like Dum-E from Iron Man). Engaging interaction through emotional expression — reacts to your mood.

Project Overview

The System

A real-time AI-powered robotic manipulator that detects human faces, continuously tracks them, recognizes the person's emotional state through deep learning, stabilizes the emotion through a multi-stage filtering pipeline, and physically expresses context-appropriate behavior through expressive servo motion and an OLED facial display.

  • Detects faces in real-time (YOLOv8, GPU-accelerated)
  • Tracks face continuously (arm follows you)
  • Recognizes emotions (DeepFace, 7 base emotions)
  • Stabilizes through 3-layer pipeline (filter → derive → vote)
  • Expresses emotion through full-body poses (3 servos)
  • Displays emotion face on OLED (pixel-art expressions)

Two Simultaneous Loops

Both run every frame. The robot watches you AND reacts to your mood simultaneously.

1
Loop 1 — Physical Tracking
Camera → Face Position → Error Calculation → Servo Motion
The arm physically follows the user's face in real-time.
2
Loop 2 — Emotional Perception
Camera → Face Crop → Emotion AI → 3-Layer Filter → Behavior Adaptation
The arm adapts its posture and expression based on detected emotion.
04

System Architecture

End-to-End Pipeline

1
Webcam captures live video frames from an external 1080p camera.
2
YOLOv8 detects the face and outputs two things: face crop (for emotion analysis) + face center coordinates (for tracking).
3
DeepFace receives the face crop and predicts 7 emotion probabilities (happy, sad, angry, surprise, neutral, fear, disgust).
4
EmotionFilter (Layer 1) — removes noise, suppresses false positives (fear/disgust), confidence gate ≥ 60%, dominance margin ≥ 15%.
5
DerivedEmotionEngine (Layer 2) — creates compound emotions: EXCITED (happy > 0.6 AND surprise > 0.2) and BORED (neutral > 0.9 for 5+ seconds).
6
EmotionStateMachine (Layer 3) — majority voting over sliding window of 12 frames, threshold of 7 votes. Produces one stable final emotion.
7
TrackerController blends tracking angles with emotion poses into final servo commands. Sends via USB serial to Arduino.
8
Arduino drives 3 servos (Base, Shoulder, Elbow) for arm movement and renders emotion faces on the OLED display.

Methodology

Face Detection — YOLOv8n-face

Model: YOLOv8n-face (Ultralytics), trained specifically for face detection. GPU-accelerated at ~30 FPS on RTX 3050.

Output per frame:

  • Bounding box: x1, y1, x2, y2
  • Face center: face_x, face_y (used for tracking)
  • Cropped face image (sent to emotion model)
  • Mirror-corrected (horizontal flip for natural interaction)

Emotion Recognition — DeepFace

Model: DeepFace with VGG-Face backend. Key optimization: detector_backend = "skip" — since YOLO already detected the face, DeepFace skips re-detection and directly classifies.

7 base emotions detected: happy, sad, angry, surprise, neutral, fear*, disgust*

*fear and disgust are suppressed — they are almost always false positives on webcam input.

3-Layer Emotion Stability Pipeline

Raw predictions are noisy — our pipeline makes them reliable.

L1
EmotionFilter — Suppresses fear & disgust (false positives). Confidence gate ≥ 60% (rejects weak predictions). Dominance margin ≥ 15% (rejects confused results). Neutral suppression — swaps to 2nd place if close.
L2
DerivedEmotionEngine — Creates compound emotions DeepFace can't detect:
EXCITED: happy > 0.6 AND surprise > 0.2 (simultaneously)
BORED: neutral > 0.9 for 5+ continuous seconds
Demonstrates temporal reasoning, not just per-frame classification.
L3
EmotionStateMachine — Majority voting over a sliding window of 12 frames with a threshold of 7 votes. Emotion must appear 7/12 times before the robot switches. If input is None → holds last-known-good emotion. Output: one stable final_emotion string.

Face Tracking

The arm physically follows the user's face using error-based proportional control:

  • error_x = face_x − frame_center_x
  • error_y = face_y − frame_center_y
  • base = CENTER + error_x × GAIN_X
  • shoulder = CENTER − error_y × GAIN_Y

Anti-Jitter Measures:

  • Deadzone: 8 pixels — tiny movements are ignored
  • EMA Smoothing (α = 0.3) — output = 0.3 × new + 0.7 × previous
  • Minimum angle change: 2° — skip updates smaller than this
  • Rate limiting: Max ~12 commands/sec (80ms interval)

Offset Calibration: Camera is on laptop, robot is on desk — they may not be aligned. Error-based relative tracking only cares about deviation from center. BASE_CENTER offset handles misalignment.

Behavior Blending — Tracking + Emotion

The robot watches you AND reacts to your mood simultaneously.

BASE SERVO
Tracking + Emotion Bias
Always follows face left/right. Emotion adds subtle directional lean (e.g. ANGRY → +20° lean). Formula: base = tracking + emotion_offset
SHOULDER SERVO
20% Tracking + 80% Emotion
Blends face following with emotion posture. Tracking keeps it responsive. Emotion shifts the overall posture. Formula: shoulder = lerp(tracking, emotion, 0.8)
ELBOW SERVO
100% Emotion
Purely driven by detected emotion — gesture intensity and expression. This is where personality shows. Formula: elbow = emotion_pose_elbow

OLED Emotion Face Display

The robot physically wears its emotion. A 128×64 SSD1306 OLED mounted on the arm renders pixel-art faces corresponding to each detected emotion:

  • HAPPY — Round eyes + upward smile curve
  • SAD — Round eyes + downward frown curve
  • ANGRY — Eyes with slanted angry eyebrows + flat mouth
  • SURPRISE — Wide eyes + open O-shaped mouth
  • BORED — Half-line eyes + flat mouth
  • NEUTRAL — Normal eyes + straight line mouth

Arduino Control Strategy

The Arduino firmware implements a smooth-motion-with-velocity-kick algorithm. Each servo moves toward its target with two speed zones:

  • Far zone (distance > 15°): Fast step of 4° per cycle — rapid response
  • Near zone (distance ≤ 15°): Slow step of 1° per cycle — precision settling

10 ms delay between steps ensures smooth mechanical motion. All servo values are clamped to hardware-safe limits before writing.

Conclusion

This project demonstrates that emotional perception can serve as an additional sensing modality for robotic manipulators, enabling more natural and context-aware human-robot interaction.

The system combines real-time face detection, multi-stage emotion stabilization, continuous face tracking, and expressive servo control into a cohesive, reliable, and demo-ready platform.

From task-aware robots → to context-aware robots.

Future: IK-Based Tracking

Replace proportional control with a full inverse kinematics solver — map face pixel coordinates to 3D workspace points.

Future: Multi-Face Priority

Detect and prioritize multiple faces, tracking the closest or most emotionally active subject.

Future: Trajectory Planning

Cubic spline interpolation in joint space between emotion poses for more natural, fluid arm movements.

Future: ROS 2 Integration

Port to ROS 2 for standardized message passing and integration with simulation tools like Gazebo.
